Trust Mechanics by Agreement

Segregated by Trust

Each trust operates differently. Rules below are specific to that trust — do not assume mechanics from one apply to another.

Sutton National & Sutton Specialty (SNIC / SSIC)

Comerica · Both Executed Dec 2025 · Identical Language

Trustee: Comerica Bank & Trust, N.A. · Governing law: Michigan · Regulatory reference: Oklahoma insurance law · All mechanics below apply equally to both SNIC and SSIC — trust agreements are word-for-word identical.

Investment Direction

Authorization
Bay Harbor Re written approval required. Investment manager directions flow through as designated advisor. No per-trade Beneficiary consent needed. § 3.1 / 3.3
Trustee compliance duty
None. Trustee will not verify assets meet Permitted Investment requirements. § 3.1
Unclear instructions
Assets held uninvested up to 30 calendar days pending resolution. § 3.4

Withdrawals & Income

Beneficiary withdrawal
Written Withdrawal Notice. Bay Harbor Re has 15 business days to object. No objection = deemed consent. § 2.9(a)/(b)
Income withdrawal
Bay Harbor Re can pull interest, dividends, realized gains by written request at any time. No Beneficiary consent needed. Unlimited frequency. § 5.1
Asset substitutions
Grantor written request + Beneficiary written consent. Replacement must be ≥ market value. § 3.1

Reporting & Valuation

Monthly statements
Issued within 20 days of month-end. 60-day objection window — after which deemed approved. § 2.6
Deposit/withdrawal notices
Trustee notifies both parties within 10 days. Access via Comerica Trust Online. § 2.8
Pricing
Fixed income matrix-priced. Not always real-time. Trustee has no liability for inaccuracies. § 3.5

Capital Actions & Custody

Capital actions
Grantor authorization only required. Trustee will attempt to notify Bay Harbor Re of voluntary actions but is not obligated. No authorization = no action, no liability. § 6.6
Custody
Comerica Bank & Trust, N.A. Assets held in Fed Book-Entry or DTCC. All assets must be in fully negotiable form and maintained in the US. § 6.1

Clear Blue (CBIC, CBSIC, Rock Ridge, Highlander)

Fifth Third Bank · Executed Feb–Mar 2026

Trustee: Fifth Third Bank, N.A. (successor by merger to Comerica Bank & Trust, N.A.) · Governing law: Texas / situs Michigan · Regulatory reference: Texas insurance law

Investment Direction — Stricter Than Sutton

Authorization required
Requires both Grantor written instructions AND prior written consent of Beneficiary for each investment. Unlike Sutton where Grantor direction alone is sufficient. § 5
Joint Beneficiary consent
All four entities (Clear Blue Insurance, Clear Blue Specialty, Rock Ridge, Highlander) must jointly provide written consent. Every investment and capital action needs all four. § 34
Trustee compliance duty
None. Trustee will not verify permitted investment compliance. Same as Sutton.

Withdrawals & Income

Beneficiary withdrawal
Sole discretion, any time, without advance notice to Bay Harbor Re. No 15-day objection window. Immediate upon written demand. § 10
Income withdrawal
Bay Harbor Re can pull income (dividends, interest) by written request without further Beneficiary authorization. Unlimited frequency. § 12
Asset substitutions
Grantor written request + Beneficiary written consent. Replacement ≥ market value. Beneficiary consent not to be unreasonably withheld. § 5(a)(2)

Reporting & Valuation

Monthly statements
Issued within 20 days of month-end. Shows all assets, earnings, losses, fair market value. Deposit/withdrawal notices within 10 days.
Pricing
Matrix-priced fixed income. Same methodology and Trustee non-liability as Sutton accounts.
Inspection rights
Texas DOI and other state commissioners have explicit inspection rights over trust books and assets under this agreement — broader than Sutton.

Capital Actions & Custody

Capital actions
Grantor instruction + Beneficiary written consent required — unlike Sutton (Grantor only). No action without both. § 6 (equivalent)
Custody
Fifth Third Bank, N.A. Assets held in Fed Book-Entry or DTCC. All assets in fully negotiable form, maintained in the US.

Absolute Prohibitions — All Trust Agreements

Securities lending — no repo, pledge, or lending of trust assets. Explicit in Sutton; consistent across all structures. Exhibit A (SNIC)
Affiliate / parent / subsidiary securities — no securities issued by or held in a parent, subsidiary, or affiliate of Bay Harbor Re or any Beneficiary. All agreements. § 2.1 (SNIC)
Trust assets for Trustee fees — Trustee fees paid by Bay Harbor Re outside the trust. All agreements explicitly prohibit using trust assets for this purpose.
Equity / common stock — not a permitted investment in any agreement.
Sub-investment-grade purchases — Sutton floor is BBB-/Baa3; Clear Blue floor is BBB. No purchases below each agreement's respective floor.
Non-USD securities — all securities must be USD-denominated, maintained in the United States.
All § references correspond to the trust agreement labeled in each section. SNIC and SSIC are identical. Clear Blue and Accredited both require Beneficiary consent for investments and capital actions — materially stricter than Sutton. Trisura and Accredited should not receive trades until exhibits are finalized.
